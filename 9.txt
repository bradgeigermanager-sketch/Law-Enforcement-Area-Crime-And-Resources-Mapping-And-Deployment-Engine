## 3. Operational Mechanics

* Spatio-Infrastructure Proximity Matrix (Tab 1): Instead of treating risk values in isolation, the architecture calculates a spatial distance matrix between physical sensor nodes ($X_{sensor}$) and forecasted crime coordinates ($Y_{grid}$). If an area is projected to have high incident activity but lacks active, functioning sensor footprints within a $0.015$-degree threshold (~1.6km grid distance matrix), the engine flags that zone as a CRITICAL BLIND SPOT.
* Constrained Simplex Optimizations (Tab 2): Real-world operations cannot simply deploy units to the highest risk value if that deployment pattern leaves unmonitored sectors completely empty. By dynamically updating the lower bounds of our linear programming engine:
$$\text{If Status} = \text{'CRITICAL BLIND SPOT'} \implies \text{Lower Bound} \ge \text{min\_per\_blind\_spot}$$ 
The optimization solver shifts personnel to balance coverage, ensuring physical units patrol areas where fixed sensor hardware cannot see.
